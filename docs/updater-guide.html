One-Time Web Update

A simple guide to updating your Tidesy from your computer — just this once

We're sorry for the inconvenience

Who needs to do this? Only Tidesy owners running version 3.0.1 or lower. If your device is already on version 3.0.2 (or newer), you can stop reading — your device will keep itself up to date automatically. You can check your version on the device's Configuration screen.

Tidesy is designed to update itself automatically over the internet. Unfortunately, our file hosting provider is holding on to an old copy of our update list and refuses to refresh it, which means your device cannot see the new version on its own.

To work around this, we've built a simple one-time update tool you can use from your computer. Once your device is on version 3.0.2, automatic updates will work as designed, and you'll never need to do this again.

Thank you for your patience!

Where do I go?

The updater lives on a single web page. You can open it from any computer:

Open the Tidesy Updater →

Or type this address into your browser:
bernhardkohlmeier.github.io/tidesy-firmware-releases/updater/

What you'll need

Just two things, both of which you probably already have:

💻

A computer

Windows, Mac, or Chromebook. Doesn't need to be fancy.

A USB cable

The same kind you use to charge most phones and tablets — USB-C on the Tidesy end.

Which web browser?

This is important — the updater only works in certain browsers. Use one of these:

✓ Google Chrome ✓ Microsoft Edge

These browsers will not work for the updater:

✗ Safari ✗ Firefox ✗ Mobile browsers (phones/tablets)
Don't have Chrome or Edge? Both are free. Chrome is at google.com/chrome and Edge comes with every Windows computer. Either one is fine — pick whichever you prefer.

Step-by-step

Follow these in order. The whole thing takes about three minutes.

1

Plug Tidesy into your computer

Take your USB cable and connect one end to your computer's USB port and the other end to the USB-C port on your Tidesy device. (See the manual if you're not sure where the USB-C port is on your model.)

You should see Tidesy power on and start its normal boot screen. That's fine — leave it plugged in.

2

Open the updater page in Chrome or Edge

Click the big blue button above, or type bernhardkohlmeier.github.io/tidesy-firmware-releases/updater/ into your browser's address bar.

You should see a page titled "Tidesy Firmware Updater".

3

Choose your Tidesy model

There's a dropdown labeled "Board model". Open it and pick the one that matches the screen size of your Tidesy:

  • Elecrow CrowPanel 5.0" (CROW5) — the 5-inch model
  • Elecrow CrowPanel 7.0" (CROW7) — the 7-inch model

If you're not sure, look at the label on the back of the device, or just measure the screen diagonally.

4

Click the "Install Tidesy" button

A small window will pop up asking which serial port to use. This is where it gets slightly technical — but only slightly. Read the next section for details.

5

Pick your Tidesy from the list

You'll see something like "USB Serial Device (COM5)" or "Silicon Labs CP210x...". Click that one, then click "Connect".

If you see more than one option, the right one is usually the one that appeared after you plugged Tidesy in. Unplug Tidesy briefly and watch which entry disappears — that's the one.

6

Wait for the update to finish

The browser will show a progress bar. It takes about 1-2 minutes. Don't unplug anything, don't close the browser, and don't let your computer go to sleep.

When it's done, your Tidesy will restart on its own and boot up with the new version. That's it — you're done!

What's a "COM port" anyway?

The short answer

A COM port (or "serial port") is just a name your computer gives to a device that's connected over USB and wants to talk back and forth. When you plug Tidesy in, your computer assigns it a name like COM3, COM5, or on a Mac, something like /dev/cu.usbserial-1410.

You don't need to know which exact name it has — the updater will show you a list, and you just pick the one that belongs to Tidesy.

How USB and "COM" fit together

USB is the cable. COM is the conversation. When Tidesy is plugged in, a tiny chip inside pretends to be an old-fashioned serial connection so your computer can send the new firmware to it. That "pretend serial connection" is what shows up as a COM port. It's not anything you have to set up — Windows, Mac, and Chromebook all handle it automatically.

If nothing shows up in the list: Try a different USB cable. Some USB cables are "charge only" and don't carry data. A cable that works to transfer files from a phone to a computer will always work for Tidesy too.

After the update

You're all set! Once Tidesy is on version 3.0.2, it will check for updates automatically over your home Wi-Fi the next time it restarts. You should never need to use the web updater again.

Your Wi-Fi password, station settings, brightness, and everything else are preserved across the update — nothing is erased.

Common questions

Will this delete my settings? No. Your Wi-Fi credentials, tide stations, alarm levels, and color preferences are all kept. The update only replaces the software, not your saved settings.
Is it safe to unplug Tidesy from the wall during the update? It will get its power from the USB cable while connected to the computer, so you don't need to keep it plugged into the wall. Just don't unplug the USB cable during the update.
I clicked Install but no popup appeared. Make sure you're using Chrome or Edge on a desktop or laptop — not a phone, tablet, Safari, or Firefox. The popup is blocked in browsers that don't support direct USB access.
The popup is empty or my Tidesy isn't listed. Try these in order:
1. Unplug the USB cable, wait 5 seconds, plug it back in.
2. Try a different USB cable (some are charge-only).
3. Try a different USB port on your computer.
4. Restart your computer and try again.
The update failed partway through. Don't panic — just unplug, plug back in, and try again. The updater is safe to run as many times as you need to.
Can I use my phone instead of a computer? Unfortunately, no. Phones and tablets don't allow web pages to talk to USB devices the way desktops and laptops do. You need a computer for this one-time step.
I don't have a computer at all. What do I do? Reach out to us through tidesy.com and we'll help you find a solution.
